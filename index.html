Biography

Wayne (Kuo-Wei) Lee is currently a graduate student at Carnegie Mellon University - Silicon Valley pursuing the M.S. in Software Engineering. He is interested in understanding technical perspectives of product development and building technical solutions to real-world problems. He used to work in several AI startups on practical AI deployment missions regarding person re-identification, price trending prediction, applied machine learning on mobile phones, and deep SLAM on drone. Furthermore, he had experienced iOS app designing concerning food diary with 10k+ daily users and the backend system development as well. Besides his industry experience, he also had research experiences on computer vision and deep learning when he was a research assistant at National Tsing Hua University. The research topics include but not limited to 3D hand pose estimation and 3D voxel-based Map reconstruction.

During his study at CMU, he started introducing software engineering principles and practices with application to a series of increasingly complex systems challenges drawn from the real world. For example, he succeeded in the courses of Reliable Distributed Systems and Machine Learning for Engineers, which lays hands on many practical projects (e.g. Fault-Tolerance System, COVID-19 Analysis). Most importantly, he's always passionate about learning and creating interesting stuff.

"If coding is a magic, he's willing to be the magician who brings the world happiness." His résumé is available. Please feel free to contact him via e-mail or LinkedIn at any time!
